The Challenge

A Trusted Local Supplier Needed Stronger Digital Proof

Jones Plumbing Plus had the kind of local relevance many businesses are trying to build: a physical showroom, practical staff support, a wide product range, and a customer base that includes renovators, homeowners, plumbers, builders, and trade buyers. The business was not trying to win attention with a shallow message. It needed the online ecosystem to reflect the depth of what customers could actually get from the team.

The challenge was that local discovery now happens before a customer walks into the showroom. A homeowner searching for tapware, a builder looking for plumbing supplies, or a renovator comparing bathroom products may look at Google Search, Google Maps, reviews, Facebook, and website pages before they make contact. If those signals are unclear or under-developed, the business can lose demand even when the service quality is strong.

There was also a structural problem specific to independent plumbing suppliers. National chains like Reece have large marketing budgets, national brand recognition, and dedicated SEO teams. An independent Plumbing Plus member cannot outspend them. But it can out-structure them. The question was whether Jones Plumbing Plus had the right visibility architecture in place to be found, understood, and chosen ahead of the national competition.

The answer, when Reviewly began the audit, was no. The website had indexing issues that prevented key pages from being read reliably by Google. The Google Business Profiles across Albury and Corowa were under-developed. Review activity had been quiet since 2024. Social content was inconsistent. And the business was spending on traditional media - TV and radio - that could not be tracked, measured, or tied to any increase in foot traffic or sales enquiries.

"Jones Plumbing Plus did not need to become a different business. It needed its existing trust to become clearer across the surfaces customers already use: Google Search, Google Business Profile, Facebook, website pages, reviews, and location-based discovery."

The Solution

Turning Local Product Knowledge Into Visibility Architecture

Reviewly approached Jones Plumbing Plus as a local entity and trust structure project. Traditional local SEO is not enough when customers and search systems need proof across multiple surfaces. The work had to connect the website, Google Business Profile, search data, review activity, social education, and location performance into one clearer system.

The first priority was to make the business easier to recognise. Jones Plumbing Plus needed to be understood as a plumbing supplies and showroom business serving both DIY and trade customers - not confused with a generic plumber or a thin local listing. That distinction matters because the customer intent is different. Someone searching for plumbing supplies, tapware, sinks, bathroom products, or showroom advice needs a business that can supply, guide, and support the decision.

The indexing work protected the website foundation. When indexing issue fixes initially failed, Reviewly treated that as a visibility risk and worked through the fixes so key pages could be read, validated, and positioned more reliably. Strong content cannot perform if search systems are struggling to process it.

Review response and fresh review activity rebuilt the trust layer. The new review added during the program gave the business current proof after a long gap. The response reinforced the family business positioning, the showroom range, and the helpful staff experience - without turning the reply into keyword stuffing.

Social media became part of the authority layer. Consistent educational content helped readers understand products, renovation choices, and showroom value. This gave the brand a steadier public presence and helped connect the physical showroom experience with what people see online.

Search Console Results

90 Keywords Moving Together: What Average Position Really Means

The average position improvement from 25.5 to 18.1 is often misread as a modest result. It is not. Here is why the context matters.

This is a fleet-wide movement, not a single keyword win.

Jones Plumbing Plus tracks 90 keywords across its Albury and Corowa locations. The average position of 18.1 is the mean across all 90 keywords simultaneously. When the average moves from 25.5 to 18.1, it means the entire keyword fleet shifted upward by 7.4 positions on average. That is dozens of keywords moving from page 2-3 into page 1-2 at the same time.

The positions distribution chart below shows this clearly. At the start of May, the majority of keywords were sitting in the yellow and orange bands (positions 11-30). By June 2, the green bands (positions 1-5) have grown significantly and are now the dominant colour across the top of the chart. The grey and red bands (positions 51-100+) have shrunk. This is a 31-day improvement across the entire keyword set.
