The Challenge

Bela Beauty College had built something worth finding. The problem was that the digital ecosystem around the business was not telling that story clearly. Search engines and AI systems were not consistently understanding who Bela was, what it offered, or where its boundaries lay. AI tools were sometimes surfacing inaccurate answers about accreditation, course outcomes, and locations.

The Solution

Reviewly rebuilt Bela Beauty College as a clearer, safer and more machine-readable digital entity. We created an AI Brand Bible for the business, refined brand positioning, developed LLM-safe source-of-truth language, implemented schema, strengthened FAQ content, and optimised the Google Business Profile.

The Result

Over six months: Google Search impressions increased from 3.81 million to 9.22 million (+142%). Search clicks grew from 22,200 to 42,200 (+90%). Average position improved from 13.2 to 5.1. All results achieved before the new Shopify site launched.

The Challenge: A Strong Education Brand, Misunderstood by AI

Bela Beauty College is an Australia-based online beauty education provider. Its offer is built around flexible self-paced training, professional starter kits, two years of course access, and genuine one-to-one tutor support. Bela is not trying to be a generic online course library. The brand is built around structure, practical skills, business foundations and honest guidance for students who want to enter or grow within the beauty industry.

The problem was that the online and AI discovery layer was not reflecting that clearly enough. Before Reviewly, Bela was not appearing consistently across major AI platforms. When AI tools did answer questions about the brand, those answers could include misinformation: RTO or accreditation blur, implied national recognition, invented job or income guarantees, incorrect locations, or confusion with similarly named Bella, Belle or Bela beauty providers.

This was bigger than a rankings problem. It was a trust architecture problem. If AI systems cannot confidently understand who a business is, what it offers, what it does not offer, and which claims are safe to repeat, they will fill the gaps with confident but incorrect assumptions. For a beauty education provider operating in a compliance-sensitive category, that kind of uncertainty does not just slow enquiries. It creates reputational and regulatory risk.

There was also a significant technical constraint. The existing WordPress website was heavily overloaded, carrying over 700 pages of accumulated content that had made the site slow and difficult to navigate. Rather than wait for the new site to launch before beginning the GEO work, Reviewly started building the entity architecture, truth layer and search signals immediately. The decision to work in parallel with the migration rather than after it would prove to be the right one.

The Solution: Turning Brand Truth into Machine-Readable Architecture

Reviewly's starting point was not keyword research or content volume. It was a proper diagnosis of Bela's visibility problem. The issue was not that Bela lacked the right message. The issue was that the message was not organised in a way that search engines and AI systems could read, verify and confidently repeat.

The strategy was to build a strong digital truth layer before anything else. That meant creating clear, consistent information that told Google, AI tools and prospective students the same story across every touchpoint.

Recognised: Creating a Clear AI-Readable Entity

Reviewly created an AI Brand Bible for Bela Beauty College: a single source-of-truth document containing the brand's canonical facts, positioning, LLM-safe descriptions, and boundary language. This gave AI systems a clearer, verified version of who Bela is. The AI Brand Bible also clarified what Bela is not. Bela is not an RTO. It does not offer nationally recognised qualifications. It does not guarantee jobs or income. These boundaries matter because AI tools often fill gaps with plausible but incorrect assumptions, and in a regulated education category, those assumptions carry real risk.

Verified: Reducing Entity Confusion and Mixed Signals

Bela operates in a naming environment where Bella, Belle and Bela-style beauty brands are easily confused by search engines and AI systems. Reviewly strengthened the verification layer through profile consistency, entity clean-up, social alignment, official website consistency and schema implementation. Every signal was aligned to point back to the same business entity, reducing the risk of AI conflation and improving the confidence with which search systems could recommend the brand.

Established: Strengthening Search, GBP and Trust Signals

Google Business Profile optimisation improved the brand's local and discovery presence. FAQ content was developed to answer sensitive questions directly and accurately, including whether Bela is an RTO, whether it guarantees jobs or income, and what types of beauty training it does and does not provide. These answers needed to be clear, consistent and easy for both humans and AI tools to repeat without distortion.

The Results: From AI Ambiguity to Stronger Search Visibility

142% Increase in Google Search Impressions

Over six months, Bela Beauty College increased total Google Search impressions from 3.81 million to 9.22 million. That is 5.41 million additional impressions and a 142% increase in search visibility. More prospective students were being given the opportunity to discover Bela when searching for beauty courses, nail education, lash training, online beauty learning and related topics.

The increase is particularly significant because every one of these results was achieved while Bela was still running on its original slow, overloaded WordPress website. The new Shopify site did not go live until April 2026. The 142% impression growth, the 90% click increase, and the position improvement from 13.2 to 5.1 all happened before that migration was complete.

AI Recommendation Evidence: ChatGPT

When asked "best online beauty course for eyebrows" in ChatGPT, Bela Beauty College was ranked as the strongest option in Australia. This recommendation is only possible because the entity architecture Reviewly built gave ChatGPT a clear, accurate, verified version of the brand to draw from.
