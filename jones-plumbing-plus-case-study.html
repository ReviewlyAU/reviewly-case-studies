The Situation
Where Jones Plumbing Plus Started
A 73-Year Business That Google Could Not Find
Jones Plumbing Plus is not a new business. It has served the Corowa region for 73 years - supplying plumbers, builders, renovators and homeowners with everything from tapware and fixtures to full bathroom and kitchen showroom support. When the business opened a new showroom in Albury, the team did what most established businesses do. They invested in a grand opening. TV ads. Radio ads. A new website.
None of it moved the needle on foot traffic or sales enquiries. The reason was not the product. The reason was not the team. The reason was that Google had no idea who Jones Plumbing Plus was in Albury - and neither did any AI system, directory, or local discovery surface that a customer might use before walking through the door.

"Despite investing in TV and radio ads for their grand opening and launching a new website, Jones Plumbing Plus was struggling to attract local customers to their new Albury showroom. Google had no idea who they were or what they sold."

The Core Problem

Jones Plumbing Plus had 73 years of real-world trust. The business had product range, staff knowledge, and a genuine showroom offer. The problem was that none of that trust existed in a form that Google, Maps, or AI systems could read, validate, or recommend. The digital structure was invisible. The Google Business Profile was unverified. The keyword cluster was near empty. The website had indexing issues that meant key pages were not being read by search systems at all.

The business was also competing in a category where customers often call directly from a Google Business Profile result - before they visit a website, before they compare prices, before they walk in. If the profile is not visible, those calls go to a competitor. In Albury, at the starting point, they were going to plumbers.
What Was Missing
  • Google Business Profile not verified or actively managed
  • Incorrect business category code assigned
  • Website indexing issues blocking key pages from Google
  • Near-empty keyword cluster - no local commercial terms
  • No current review activity since 2024
  • No consistent social content or educational presence
What Was Already Strong
  • 73 years of real-world operational history
  • Physical showroom with genuine product range
  • Staff knowledge and trade buyer relationships
  • Two locations - Albury and Corowa
  • Established community trust in Corowa region

The Approach
How Reviewly Applied the REVIEW Method
Turning 73 Years of Real Trust Into Visible Trust
The REVIEW Method is Reviewly's proprietary framework for building brand visibility that earns trust and recommendation in modern search environments. For Jones Plumbing Plus, the work was not about creating something new. It was about making what already existed legible to Google, Maps, AI systems, and the customers who use them.
R Recognised Clarified Jones Plumbing Plus as a local plumbing supply, showroom and trade support business across Albury and Corowa. Corrected the business category code. Established clear positioning for both locations. Search systems and customers could now understand what the business does, who it serves, and where it operates - reducing the misclassification that had been costing them local ranking.
E Established Strengthened Google Business Profile activity, social posting, website content pathways and local performance monitoring for both Albury and Corowa locations. The business showed active, operational signals across the surfaces customers use before contacting a supplier - not just a passive listing.
V Verified Worked through page indexing issues, supported location-specific GBP evidence and kept business information aligned across discovery surfaces. Addressed indexing failures that had been blocking key pages. Reduced friction for Google and customers by making the business easier to validate. Key commercial pages became readable and rankable.
I Influential Reactivated visible review momentum. Supported review response strategy. Continued educational social content that demonstrated product knowledge and showroom value. Customers gained fresher proof that real people are still choosing and engaging with the business. Trust score moved from 3.6 to 4.6 within 30 days.
E Enduring Created an ongoing system for monitoring search, GBP calls, profile interactions, social presence, review activity and indexing health across both locations. Built a foundation that compounds across Albury, Corowa and surrounding search demand - not a one-time fix that fades.
The Key Insight

Jones Plumbing Plus was not only competing for rankings. It was competing for clarity at the point of local decision-making. A customer may see the Business Profile, the Facebook page, a review reply, a product post or a search result before they ever walk into the showroom. Once the business information, profile actions, review response, educational content and indexing work began pointing in the same direction, the brand became easier to understand and easier to act on.

What the Impression Drop Actually Means

A drop in impressions alongside an improvement in position and CTR is a positive signal. It means the website stopped appearing for broad, irrelevant queries and started appearing more reliably for the specific commercial terms that drive calls and showroom visits. The search footprint became more precise, not smaller.
